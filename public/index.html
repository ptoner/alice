Alice's Adventures in Wonderland
By Lewis Carroll & Patrick Toner

The most copied art project of all time!

Okay, so you're thinking, "Do I really want to read a book that's over a hundred years old? Isn't it going to be kind of boring?" Well, I'm here to tell you that Alice's Adventures in Wonderland is not your average, stuffy old book. It's actually a pretty wild ride.

Alice is a normal girl who falls asleep in a meadow and tumbles down a giant rabbit hole. She winds up in a place called Wonderland, which is full of all sorts of strange and wonderful creatures. There's the Mad Hatter, the Cheshire Cat, the Queen of Hearts...and don't even get me started on the Red Queen.

Alice has all sorts of adventures in Wonderland, and she even grows and changes as a person by the end of the book. So if you're looking for something a little bit different to read, something that will make you laugh and think and maybe even feel a little bit scared, then you should definitely check out Alice's Adventures in Wonderland.

FAQ

What are NFTs?

An NFT is a digital collectible. It's fun to collect things and NFTs allow us to collect digital items that inherit the permissionless and P2P nature of the Ethereum network. It's a big worldwide database that keeps track of the biggest fan of each piece. Just like a physical painting there can be many copies but some will just always be a little more special than others.

Why would I want to collect NFTs?

For the reasons your grandma has a closet full of your elementary school artwork. Collectibles help us remember.

Collecting an NFT creates an entry in a database but more important it creates an entry in your mind. Every time you see your NFT you'll remember the moment you collected it. You'll remember all the context around that moment. Where you were. Who you were with. How the air smelled. It brings you back. It's like a magic trick.

Why use a Creative Commons Public Domain license?

Creative Commons CC0 enables scientists, educators, artists and other creators and owners of copyright- or database-protected content to waive those interests in their works and thereby place them as completely as possible in the public domain, so that others may freely build upon, enhance and reuse the works for any purposes without restriction under copyright or database law.

There are no legal rights associated with NFT ownership and all source code is MIT Licensed. Just because an NFT displays certain information doesn't mean you own that information. That would be silly.

What do I actually own?

I'm not sure even sure own is the right word. When you own an NFT that means your Ethereum account is mapped to a particular token ID in a smart contract. This mapping gives your wallet permission to transfer the NFT to another wallet. This basic utility allows you to list the NFT on many marketplaces.

But then again what does owning something even mean? If you can't trade it you don't own it. And you can trade your NFTs.
